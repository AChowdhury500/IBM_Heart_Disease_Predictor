About This Project

Heart Disease Prediction Using Logistic Regression
Heart disease is a leading cause of death worldwide. Early detection can significantly improve treatment and outcomes. This project demonstrates a simple machine learning pipeline using logistic regression trained on generated sample data to predict the likelihood of heart disease from 11 input features.

Project Details

  • Model: Logistic Regression (scikit-learn)
  • Features: age, sex, chest pain type, resting BP, cholesterol, fasting blood sugar, resting ECG, max heart rate, exercise angina, ST depression (oldpeak), slope
  • Web: Flask app with a simple form to collect inputs and show prediction

About This Project

Heart Disease Prediction Using Logistic Regression

Clinical Parameters Used

1. Age

Patient's age in years - risk increases with age

2. Sex

Biological sex (Male/Female) - different risk profiles

3. Chest Pain Type

Type of chest pain experienced during examination

4. Resting Blood Pressure

Blood pressure measured at rest in mmHg

5. Cholesterol

Serum cholesterol level in mg/dl

6. Fasting Blood Sugar

Blood sugar levels after fasting

7. Resting ECG

Electrocardiographic results at rest

8. Maximum Heart Rate

Highest heart rate achieved during testing

9. Exercise Angina

Presence of angina induced by exercise

10. ST Depression

ST segment depression induced by exercise

11. Slope

Slope of the peak exercise ST segment
